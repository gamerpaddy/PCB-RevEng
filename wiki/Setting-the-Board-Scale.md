# Setting the Board Scale

The program needs to know the real size of the board so that footprints come out the correct size and measurements are meaningful. You set this once with the Calibrate button.

## Calibrating from a known distance

1. Find something on the board whose real length you know. A common choice is the spacing between two header pins, which is usually 2.54 millimetres, or the length of a known component.
2. Click **Calibrate** at the top of the left panel.
3. Press and drag along that known distance on the board, from one end to the other, then release.
4. A box asks for the real length. Type it in and confirm.

The board scale is now set. Footprints you place will match the photo, and the position readout at the bottom will show correct millimetre values.

## Measuring a distance

To measure something without changing the scale, use Measure.

1. Click **Measure** at the top of the left panel.
2. Press and drag from one point to the other, then release.

The distance is shown both in pixels and in your chosen unit. You can drag again to measure something else.

## Choosing millimetres or mils

Open **Options** in the top bar and use the Units dropdown to switch between millimetres and mils. This changes the position readout, the measure tool and the footprint grid.
